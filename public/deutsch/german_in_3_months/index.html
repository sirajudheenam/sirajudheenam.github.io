Learn German in 3 months

Introduction:

Studying to achieve a B1 level in German in three months is an ambitious goal that will require a structured and disciplined study schedule. Here’s a daily plan that focuses on comprehensive learning, incorporating online resources and materials:

Weeks 1-2: Foundations and Basic Vocabulary

Day 1-7: Basics and Pronunciation

Morning (30 minutes): Start with Duolingo or Babbel for basic lessons. Afternoon (30 minutes): Practice pronunciation using online tutorials or apps. Evening (30 minutes): Watch YouTube videos or podcasts for basic German conversations.

Day 8-14: Vocabulary Building

Morning (30 minutes): Continue with Duolingo or Babbel. Afternoon (30 minutes): Use Anki or Memrise for vocabulary practice. Evening (30 minutes): Read and learn new words from a beginner’s German book or website.

Weeks 3-6: Grammar and Intermediate Vocabulary

Day 15-42: Grammar Focus

Morning (30 minutes): Dedicate this time to studying grammar using resources like Deutsche Welle’s online grammar course. Afternoon (30 minutes): Complete grammar exercises and quizzes. Evening (30 minutes): Review grammar concepts covered during the day.

Day 43-84: Vocabulary Expansion

Morning (30 minutes): Continue using Anki, Memrise, or Quizlet for vocabulary. Afternoon (30 minutes): Practice reading and understanding texts in German. Evening (30 minutes): Write short sentences or paragraphs using newly acquired vocabulary.

Weeks 7-9: Listening and Speaking Practice

Day 85-105: Listening Skills

Morning (30 minutes): Listen to German podcasts or watch German TV shows with subtitles. Afternoon (30 minutes): Repeat sentences and shadow pronunciation. Evening (30 minutes): Engage in spoken exercises with language exchange partners or online tutors.

Day 106-126: Speaking Skills

Morning (30 minutes): Continue conversing with native speakers through language exchange platforms. Afternoon (30 minutes): Record yourself speaking in German and compare it to native speakers. Evening (30 minutes): Join online German conversation groups.

Weeks 10-12: Comprehensive Review and Exam Preparation

Day 127-150: Review and Practice

Morning (30 minutes): Review all grammar and vocabulary concepts. Afternoon (30 minutes): Complete practice tests and exercises from B1-level study materials (e.g., Goethe Institute practice exams). Evening (30 minutes): Fine-tune speaking and listening skills.

Day 151-180: Final Stretch

Morning (30 minutes): Focus on areas of weakness identified during practice tests. Afternoon (30 minutes): Review and revise essays or written responses. Evening (30 minutes): Stay relaxed and confident; avoid cramming.

Online Resources:

Duolingo, Babbel, or Rosetta Stone for structured lessons. Memrise, Anki, or Quizlet for vocabulary building. Deutsche Welle’s online resources for grammar and comprehension. GermanPod101 or Coffee Break German for language podcasts. YouTube channels for German learners, like “Easy German.” Language exchange platforms such as Tandem, HelloTalk, or iTalki for practice with native speakers.

Goethe Institute’s B1 practice materials and mock exams for test preparation.

Conclusion

Remember to adapt this schedule to your own pace and preferences, and be sure to dedicate some time each day to review and reinforce what you’ve learned. Consistency and practice are key to achieving your goal of reaching a B1 level in German within three months. Good luck with your studies!
